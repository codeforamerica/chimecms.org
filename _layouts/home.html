In this new world, access for all means digital.

Residents expect to find the same services online as they would find inside city hall. Further, we now know that 64% of American adults own a smartphone of some kind and many underserved citizens use a smartphone as their only means of online access. People need and expect to do city business from wherever they are.

Through our work on Digital Front Door, we're tackling the underlying processes and principles required for Governments to meet their residents where they are.
